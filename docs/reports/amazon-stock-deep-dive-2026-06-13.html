Send proven sellers (Miles Morales' Mask, Eiffel Tower, Darth Vader, Jane Goodall) plus the buried high-value Coruscant Guard Gunship (75354) to FBA to win the Prime buy box — only where margin absorbs FBA fees + storage.
  2. Clear the dead tail now (~£2.3k). Liquidate the BSR>300k stock (£1,828) and the Strictly Briks junk (£482) via eBay/BL/Vinted/bundles. Recovers cash to reinvest in Q4 winners.
  3. Win the free wins. ~44 listings have no buy-box holder and ~36 have ≤3 competitors — audit and price these to win. No FBA needed; pure upside (e.g. 42164, 75390, 72040, 10931 above).
  4. Restock velocity before October. Depth on 40xxx seasonals for volume; Eiffel/Darth Vader class for margin.
  5. Don't chase price. Margins are healthy (29.5%) and you're never above buy box. Reflexive markdowns would erode the one thing that's working.
  6. Purchasing discipline. The £700+ frozen in dead Architecture sets (UN HQ 21018, Guggenheim 21004) and the £482 non-LEGO bricks were avoidable — check BSR before buying high-value or non-LEGO.
  7. Data fix (repeatability). The pricer never captures live quantity and only samples your price; ASINs aren't yet on historical order lines' fee data. Capture quantity, FBM/FBA channel and the buy-box holder daily so a dashboard can auto-flag “buried” and “dead” stock.

How “never sold” was measured · Data window

At the ASIN level from inventory_items (one row = one physical copy): currently-listed Amazon ASINs (status='LISTED' + amazon_asin + listing_platform='amazon') matched against every ASIN that has ever appeared in your Amazon order history (order_items.item_number, which carries the ASIN, joined to platform_orders). “Never sold” = a listed ASIN that never appears in that order history.

The first pass used the inventory-linked sales window (from ~9 Jan 2026 only). Extending to the full raw Amazon order history already in the system (back to 31 Jan 2025, ~17 months) reclassified just 8 ASINs from “never sold” to “sold” (139 → 136) and barely moved the dead-stock capital (£1,863 → £1,828) — so the conclusions are unchanged. Data before Jan 2025 (and beyond Amazon SP-API's ~24-month retention) isn't retrievable, but given the negligible shift it would not change the picture.

Caveats

• It's Amazon-specific; a few never-sold-on-Amazon sets do sell on eBay/BrickLink.
• BSR is a point-in-time rank — treat bands as demand tiers, not exact velocity.
• Margin uses a flat £3/£4 postage estimate; large/heavy sets cost more.
• Competitive data is fresh (234/246 live ASINs priced ≤2 days ago); platform_listings is a March snapshot used only for cross-checks (21 live ASINs showed “Inactive” there — worth a Seller-Central listing-health pass).
